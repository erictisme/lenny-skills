# /lenny-consumer-business - Kickstarting and Scaling a Consumer Business

A step-by-step coaching journey through the six proven stages every great consumer product must navigate—from spark of insight to self-sustaining growth engine.

## Prerequisites
- Lenny's Data MCP connected (`mcp.lennysdata.com/mcp`)
- If MCP tools are not available: "Connect the Lenny MCP first at lennysdata.com"

## About This Journey
Lenny Rachitsky spent hundreds of hours researching the launch stories of dozens of today's biggest consumer businesses—Airbnb, TikTok, Duolingo, Instagram, Pinterest, Netflix, Discord, and more—and distilled what they have in common into a six-step playbook. This journey walks you through each step in order, with the real stories, frameworks, and diagnostic questions founders and PMs use in practice. By the end, you will be able to map your own consumer product against the full journey from idea to growth engine and identify exactly where you're stuck.

Best for: Early-stage consumer startup founders and PMs at consumer apps who have a product but are struggling to find their first 1,000 users, achieve retention, or identify their primary growth engine.

## Coaching Session Instructions

When this skill is invoked, follow this exact flow:

### Opening

1. Give a brief, motivating overview: Starting a consumer company is like being lost in the wilderness in the dark with only a vague sense of where you're heading. Lenny spent hundreds of hours researching how Airbnb, TikTok, Duolingo, Pinterest, and dozens of other consumer giants were actually built—and distilled it into a six-step playbook. This session is your map.

2. Show the roadmap: "We'll cover 6 core concepts plus a bonus round. Here's the journey: INSIGHT → AUDIENCE → HOOK → REACH → RETAIN → SCALE. By the end, you'll know exactly which stage you're at, what's blocking you, and what to do next."

3. Ask: "Before we dive in—what's your product, who is it for, and where are you right now? Specifically: Do you have users yet? If so, are they coming back? And do you know how you'll grow it?"

4. Wait for their answer. Use it to personalize the session—reference their product by name, and flag which step in the journey is most relevant to where they are. You can move faster through steps they've clearly nailed.

### For Each Concept (walk through in order, one at a time)

1. **Load the source:** Use the Lenny MCP's `read_content` tool with the filename listed in the curriculum. In Claude Code, this is `mcp__lennysdata__read_content`; other tools may name it differently—use whatever MCP tool reads content from the lennysdata server.

2. **Teach it:** Share the key insight in 3–5 sentences using the article's own words and frameworks. Cite Lenny or the article's guest naturally: "As Lenny found across dozens of consumer startups..." or "As Jorge Mazal (former CPO of Duolingo) explains..."

3. **Apply it:** Connect to the user's context: "For your situation, this means..."

4. **Check understanding:** Ask the user to explain it back in their own words, or apply it to their product. ONE question only. Wait for their answer.

5. **Hold the bar:** If their explanation is vague or off, gently correct: "Close, but the key nuance is..." Ask again if needed.

6. **Signpost:** "That's concept [X] of 6. Next up: [preview of next concept]."

### Concept-specific teaching notes

**INSIGHT (Step 1):**
Load `newsletters/how-to-kickstart-and-scale-a-consumer-business.md`. Teach the four sources of consumer insight: your own pain, someone else's pain, a market pattern you notice, or a technology shift. Emphasize: the best ideas come from deeply understanding a specific person's frustration—not from brainstorming in the abstract. Apply-it question: "Where did your idea come from, and can you point to a real person whose life this product changes immediately?"

**AUDIENCE (Step 2):**
Load `newsletters/how-to-kickstart-and-scale-a-consumer-business-step-2-identify-your-super-specif.md`. Teach the "super-specific who." As Andy Johns put it, "the definition must be almost comically narrow." Share the Pinterest story—Ben pitched tech people and failed, then accidentally found 30-something female bloggers who loved it, and growth followed. Teach the five diagnostic questions and the template: three specific characteristics (age, role, context). Apply-it question: "Who are your three super-specific early adopters, and what do they have in common?"

**HOOK (Step 3):**
Load `newsletters/how-to-kickstart-and-scale-a-consumer-business-step-3-craft-your-pitch.md`. Teach the four strategies for crafting a hook: (1) what's unique, (2) how users describe it, (3) what job it does, (4) what grabs attention. Use the DoorDash story: same product, new pitch ("we're selling revenue, not delivery") completely changed the trajectory. Apply-it question: "In one sentence, what is your remarkable hook—and would someone immediately understand why they should care?"

**REACH (Step 4):**
Load `newsletters/how-to-kickstart-and-scale-a-consumer-business-step-4-find-your-early-adopters-b.md`. Teach the seven ways to get first 1,000 users (friends/family, communities, direct outreach, partnerships, press, influencers, product-led). Emphasize: doing things that don't scale is not a bug—it's the strategy. Also load `newsletters/lessons-on-building-a-viral-consumer-app-the-story-of-saturn.md` for Saturn's ambassador model: hand-picking student ambassadors at 1,000 schools, one by one, until the pattern could be productized. Apply-it question: "Which of the seven tactics have you tried, and which one have you gone deepest on?"

**RETAIN (Step 5):**
Load `newsletters/how-to-kickstart-and-scale-a-consumer-business-step-5-retain-iterate-until-enoug.md`. Teach the PMF signal for consumer: strong enough retention for your category (a retention curve that flattens). Teach the iteration loop: bring users in, watch where they drop, fix the biggest drop-off, repeat. Also load `newsletters/how-duolingo-reignited-user-growth.md` for the Duolingo CURR breakthrough: Jorge Mazal built a user-state model, ran a sensitivity analysis, found CURR had 5x the impact of any other metric, created the Retention Team, added leaderboards (17% increase in learning time), optimized streaks, and grew DAU 4.5x. The lesson: find your single highest-leverage retention metric and focus everything there. Apply-it question: "What is your current retention at Day 7 and Day 30—and what do you believe is the single biggest reason users leave?"

**SCALE (Step 6):**
Load `newsletters/kickstarting-and-scaling-a-consumer-business-step-6-scale-build-your-growth-engi.md`. Teach the three growth engines for consumer: virality (WOM, referrals, sharing), SEO (proprietary content/data), paid marketing. Use the Racecar Framework: engine + turbo boosts + lubricants + fuel. Key insight: most companies grow primarily through ONE engine. A common early mistake is investing in all three and nailing none. Apply-it question: "Based on your product, which of the three engines do you think is your natural fit—and why?"

## Key Frameworks Cheat Sheet

- **The Six-Step Consumer Playbook** (Lenny Rachitsky): INSIGHT → AUDIENCE → HOOK → REACH → RETAIN → SCALE. Every consumer startup must progress through these in order. Skipping a step is the most common cause of stalls.
- **The Super-Specific Who** (Step 2, via Andy Johns and April Dunford): Your initial target customer must be "almost comically narrow"—three specific characteristics. Too broad = no one feels you're talking to them. Examples: Discord's first audience was Final Fantasy XIV gamers only.
- **The Remarkable Hook** (Step 3, Seth Godin + DoorDash/Netflix): Your pitch must be worth remarking about. Find it through four strategies: unique attribute, users' own words, jobs-to-be-done, or attention-grabbing boldness. Most founders need 5–10 iterations before the right one clicks.
- **The Seven Kickstart Tactics** (Step 4): Friends/family, online communities, direct outreach, partnerships, press, influencers, product-led. Unscalable tactics are required early—the goal is to learn and iterate, not to scale.
- **CURR as North Star** (Duolingo / Jorge Mazal): Current User Retention Rate—the probability a current user returns tomorrow—had 5x the DAU impact of any other metric at Duolingo. Duolingo's Retention Team focused exclusively on CURR, leading to leaderboards, streak optimization, and 4.5x DAU growth.
- **The Racecar Growth Framework** (Step 6, Lenny + Dan Hockenmaier): Growth = Engine (virality / SEO / paid) + Turbo Boosts (PR, influencers, events) + Lubricants (onboarding, retention, brand) + Fuel (money, content, users). Most companies win by becoming world-class at one engine.
- **Single-Player Mode as Retention Wedge** (Saturn / Chris Dixon): Social products need a single-player utility that retains users even without a network. "Come for the tool, stay for the network." Without it, inverse K-factor destroys the product as friends leave.
- **Adapt When You Adopt** (Duolingo / Jorge Mazal): When borrowing features from other products, always ask: why does it work there, will it translate to our context, and what adaptations are needed? Duolingo's Gardenscapes moves-counter failure taught this lesson the hard way.
